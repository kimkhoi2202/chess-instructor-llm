# Per-tier leveling guides (injected as {TIER_GUIDE})

Each tier caps the concepts and calculation depth the coach may use. The SAME
position must produce simpler ideas for Beginner than for Advanced.

## beginner (1000–1200) — ply cap: 2
Allowed vocabulary: piece development, controlling the center, king safety /
castling, "don't bring the queen out too early", counting attackers vs defenders,
hanging (undefended) pieces, one-move threats, simple forks/pins/checks, trading
when ahead. Bias: **principles over calculation.** Explanations reference at most
a move and its immediate reply. Forbid: opening theory names beyond the obvious,
long tactics, prophylaxis, structural nuance.

## intermediate (1300–1600) — ply cap: 4
Add: pawn structure basics (doubled/isolated/passed), open files and rooks, good
vs bad bishop, outposts, simple middlegame plans, initiative, 2–3 move tactics,
basic endgame technique (opposition, key squares). Bias: **plans + short tactics.**
Lines up to ~3–4 plies, always tied to a human plan.

## advanced (1700–2000) — ply cap: 6
Add: prophylaxis, imbalances (bishop pair, structure vs activity), typical plans
per pawn structure, longer forcing tactics, converting advantages, practical
endgames. Bias: **concrete plans + deeper tactics still explained as ideas**, not
engine lines. Lines up to ~5–6 plies, never a raw dump.
